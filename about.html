About Me

Why Fresh Perspective?

Maybe you noticed my blog name or even the "fp18" in my URL ... To me, fresh perspective is the reward for thinking independently.

Take my cover picture. It's awesome, isn't it? Some far-off place full of intrigue and stories ...

Actually, it's in Kansas City! It's a well-known landmark — the WWI Memorial right across from Union Station. But thanks to fresh perspective, it could easily be the portal to another dimension.

As a redheaded left-hander, I was destined to do things differently. I started my career in print publishing, but once I learned HTML, the immediate gratification of making a correction and seeing it online in seconds resonated with me.

When I'm not writing content or researching and writing code, I'm:

  • an adoring mom
  • a cat and all-around animal lover
  • a diehard alternative music fan
  • and a semi-successful gardener

I travel whenever I can and plan to visit my beautiful daughter in August. Yep, I was just there. But this time not only Chloe will be there ... Hello, National!

June update

Guess what? I didn't code anything in May. No blog either. But June? I have plans for June.

When I got back from Seattle at the beginning of May, I jumped straight into covering for a coworker on maternity leave. (I'm a marketer with some dev responsibilities by day.) Our office is breaking into marketing automation by integrating Pardot, Sitecore and Salesforce. Guess who hadn't used two of those three tools just eight months ago? Guess who's learning their own job PLUS another using all three now?!

I've been on a fairly steady development learning track since the end of 2017, so I'm used to constantly acquiring knowledge. But when you're busy all day cramming new information in your head at work, it's hard to come home and voluntarily cram more. I did renew my SSL certificate, but that's about it. And even that took the evil THIS SITE ISN'T SAFE warning screen to overcome my inertia.

I'm Susie Multitask at work til August. But this week I'm starting a new Net Ninja series to learn about progressive web aps (PWAs). Supposedly PWAs extend mobile functionality (yay!) without learning a completely new language (double yay!) by harnessing the inherent mobile capabilities in browsers (WTF?). Sounds cool, huh? Watch for my next blog to see how it all turns out!
